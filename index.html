Research Interests

I am interested in applications of classical and quantum field theory in flat and curved space (especially in the context of black holes), and to some extent in its formal aspects particularly in relation to regularization and renormalization techniques and spectral geometry. I am a theoretician and enjoy doing analytical "pen-and-paper" work, but I also like to do numerics and learning about computers and computation. Besides quantum field theory, I am very interested in (and have been teaching for about ten years) environmental aspects of physics and how physics can help us understand and quantify the limits of sustainability and the emerging of planetary boundaries.
